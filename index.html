Divergent Creativity in Humans and Large Language Models

A computational creativity showdown between humans and AI

Read Paper View Code

Abstract

In a computational creativity showdown, some LLMs top humans in generating diverse words but fall short in writing stories and poetry. The size of generative language models does not matter, plus humans still write more divergent haikus and synopses.

Methodology

01

Divergent Association Task (DAT)

Participants generate 10 words as different from each other as possible. Semantic distance is measured using word embeddings.

100,000 human participants >500 responses per model
02

Divergent Semantic Integration (DSI)

Analyzes haiku and flash fiction creativity by measuring how diverse concepts are integrated into coherent narratives.

Multiple LLMs tested Human baselines
03

Synopsis Generation

Human-written synopses scraped from IMDB compared against LLM-generated content for creativity metrics.

Real-world data Lempel-Ziv complexity
