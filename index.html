Divergent Creativity in Humans and Large Language Models

A Computational Creativity Showdown

Read Paper View Code

Abstract

In a computational creativity showdown, some LLMs top humans in generating diverse words but fall short in writing stories and poetry. The size of generative language models does not matter, plus humans still write more divergent haikus and synopses.

Word Generation Task (DAT)

Large Language Models demonstrate superior performance in the Divergent Association Task, generating more semantically diverse word associations than human participants across 100,000+ responses.

Creative Writing

Humans maintain an edge in creative writing tasks, producing more divergent haikus and story synopses compared to LLMs, demonstrating the nuanced nature of literary creativity.

Model Size Independence

Surprisingly, the size of the language model does not correlate with creativity performance, suggesting that architectural choices and training data matter more than parameter count.

Divergent Semantic Integration

Analysis using the DSI metric reveals distinct patterns in how humans and LLMs combine semantic concepts, with implications for understanding computational creativity.

Methodology

üéØ Divergent Association Task (DAT)

The DAT measures semantic distance between generated words. We collected responses from:

  • 100,000+ human participants providing baseline creativity measures
  • 500+ responses per LLM model across various architectures
  • Multiple state-of-the-art models including GPT-3.5, GPT-4, Claude, and open-source alternatives

üìù Creative Writing Tasks

We evaluated creativity across multiple literary forms:

  • Haiku generation - Traditional 5-7-5 syllable structure
  • Flash fiction - Ultra-short story format
  • Story synopses - Human data scraped from IMDB

üî¨ Divergent Semantic Integration (DSI)

The DSI metric quantifies how diverse semantic concepts are integrated:

  • Based on semantic embedding distances (GloVe, Word2Vec)
  • Lempel-Ziv complexity analysis for linguistic patterns
  • Cross-validated with human creativity ratings
